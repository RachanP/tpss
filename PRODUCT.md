# TPSS — Teaching & Practicum Scheduling System

## Users
- **Primary**: Staff (Support Staff) and Course Heads (Makers) who spend hours managing complex block schedules.
- **Secondary**: Executives (Approvers) who need quick oversight and decision-making power.
- **Consumers**: Instructors who view their personal schedules and workloads.
- **Demographic**: University personnel of varying ages (25–65+), requiring high legibility, clear hierarchy, and intuitive navigation.

## Product Purpose
TPSS manages the highly complex "Block Schedule" and "Rotation Schedule" for the Faculty of Nursing, Mahidol University. It replaces manual/spreadsheet processes with a smart system that detects conflicts and balances instructor workloads.

## Brand Personality
**Professional · Reliable · Formal but Warm**
- ** Mahidol University Identity**: Respectful of the institution's heritage and status.
- **Academic Standard**: Use full academic titles (รศ.ดร., อ.) to maintain professional respect.
- **Trustworthy**: Every interaction should feel precise and intentional. No "whimsical" elements or excessive animations.

## Aesthetic Direction
**Professional Institutional — Minimal Data Shell**
- **Palette**: Clean Light Mode. Deep Navy (#001A3D or similar) for the primary "Chrome" (Sidebar).
- **Semantic Density**: Use high-saturation colors (Red/Yellow/Green) ONLY for status and signals.
- **Typography**: Large, readable Thai fonts (Inter/Sarabun/Outfit). High contrast for accessibility across ages.
- **Geometry**: Sharp or slightly rounded corners (2-4px for buttons, 8-10px for cards) to convey precision.
- **Anti-References**: Avoid "Consumer SaaS" trends (glassmorphism, heavy gradients, playful emojis, dark mode by default).

## Strategic Principles
1. **Data over Decoration**: Every pixel must serve the data. No decorative patterns or images.
2. **Signal over Noise**: Colors are used to highlight conflicts (Errors) or warnings, not for styling.
3. **Age-Inclusive UX**:
    - Generous touch/click targets.
    - High contrast text-to-background ratios.
    - Explicit labels instead of ambiguous icons.
    - No hidden gestures or complex hotkeys as primary navigation.
4. **Thai-First Logic**: Labels and error messages are in Thai to reduce cognitive load for local users.
